Christmas Cookies


The History of the Christmas Cookie

Christmas cookies originated in Medieval Europe as spiced, preserved treats for winter feasts, becoming popular gifts due to their long shelf life and use of prized spices like cinnamon and ginger, with the tradition brought to America by the Dutch, evolving with new cutters and recipes, culminating in the festive, decorated cookies and Santa treats we know today.


Ingredients

  • 1/4 Cup softened butter
  • 1/4 cup shortening
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 egg, seperated
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla
  • 1 cup flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup finely chopped nuts
  • Frosting

Instructions

  • Heat oven to 350 degrees
  • Mix thoroughly the butter, shortening, sugar, egg yolk, and vanilla
  • Stir in flour and salt until dough holds together
  • Shape dough by teaspoonfuls into 1-inch balls
  • Beat egg white slightly
  • Dip each ball into the egg white and roll in nuts
  • Place balls 1 inch apart on an ungreased baking sheet.
  • Press thumb deeply into the center of each ball
  • Bake about 10 minutes or until light brown
  • Immediately remove from the baking sheet and cool
  • Fill thumbprints with jelly or icing
